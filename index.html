DRUG POISONING MORTALITIES:

UNITED STATES

This dataset describes drug poisoning deaths at the U.S. at the state level by selected demographic characteristics, and includes age-adjusted death rates for drug poisoning from 1999 to 2015. Deaths are classified using the International Classification of Diseases, Tenth Revision. Drug-poisoning deaths are defined as having underlying cause-of-death codes: unintentional, suicide, homicide, or undetermined intent. Estimates are based on the National Vital Statistics System multiple cause-of-death mortality files.

Rising Death Rates

These ten states represent the highest recorded drug related deaths in the United States. This line graph shows the relationship between the death rate recorded in 1999 (shown in light blue) and the death rate recorded in 2015 (shown in navy). It shows that while the overall pattern remains the same, with California in the lead, there has been a sharp increase of drug related deaths in this sixteen year period. The recorded data highlighted the high number of deaths occuring in California, and examination shows that this pattern persists today.

Drug Related Death Distribution by State

This map shows the distribution of drug related deaths across the United States in 2015. The darkest colors show which states had a more dense amount of deaths, while the lighter color represents less deaths. The total number of drug related mortalities across the United States in 2015 was 568,842. As the map outlines, the three states with the highest death counts were California, Ohio and Pennsylvania. These three states accounted for 11,233 drug related mortalities in 2015.

California's Death Rate From 1999-2015

California Percentages

From 1999 to 2015, there were 568,842 total deaths attributed to drug related causes, and 59,427 of those were reported in California. This means that 9.5% of all drug related deaths during this time period occurred in California, making it the area with the highest percentage of deaths.

Ohio Percentages

The state with the next highest percentage of drug related deaths between 1999 and 2015 is Ohio. With 26,314 deaths attributed to drug related causes during this time period, Ohio is responsible for a mere 4.4% of these total deaths.

California Death Rise

In 1999, California had a population total of 33,499,204. In 2015, this number rose to 39,144,818. Despite only a 14% increase in total population between 1999 and 2015, California's drug related death's have risen by 175%.
